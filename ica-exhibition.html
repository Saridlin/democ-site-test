Future Democracies Laboratory @ ICA San José

An Installation by Jonathon Keats in Collaboration with Steve Durie

September 9, 2024 – February 22, 2025

How do we fix democracy? Would it help to radically reorganize government, changing the number of political parties, the composition of Congress, or the proportion of votes required to pass legislation? Could we automate governance, guiding policy by physiologically monitoring the wellbeing of the populace? What if citizenship were expanded to enfranchise all people, as well as non-human animals and plants, supporting both social and environmental justice? These were some of the core questions addressed in an immersive exhibition of the Future Democracies Laboratory at the Institute of Contemporary Art San José, which showcased some of the most provocative experiments conducted by the lab since its founding. Curated by Zoë Latzer, and comprising work by Jonathon Keats in collaboration with Steve Durie and his SJSU students, the exhibition included an interactive digital legislature, a system to poll the political opinion of houseplants using probes made out of banana, and apparatus to mutate the law by chopping up volumes of U.S. Code. The exhibition also collected public feedback on how to improve democracy in the United States.
