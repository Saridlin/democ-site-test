Democracy Laboratory Exhibition 1 @ SJSU

A Showcase of the SJSU Future For Democracies Laboratory

May 9th, 2022

This gallery show marked a milestone in sharing the range of experimental projects developed at SJSU for the Democracy Labs since 2019. It featured the debut of the first version of the Democracy Engine, whose development began before the COVID pandemic.
Presented in its initial “Beta Test” form, the Democracy Engine is a simulator of the U.S. Congress. It models participation by representatives, senators, the president, and the vice president, allowing participants to adjust the players and rules to explore how changes could affect voting outcomes. This “congressional system builder” invites users to reimagine a more effective Congress based on priorities they set.
Other featured works included a class-led project using biosensing of human stress levels to inform decision-making—pointing toward possible future voting systems that adapt to physiological cues.
The show also debuted the Banana Differential Project, which used the ripening of bananas as a proxy for gauging plant health in surrounding environments, from household plants to backyard gardens. Together, these projects embodied speculative, hands-on approaches to exploring governance, environmental sensing, and the creative intersections between technology, biology, and civic engagement.
