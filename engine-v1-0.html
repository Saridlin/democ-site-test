Democracy Engine 1.0 Development

A Web-Based Simulation

July 2nd, 2021

The Future Democracies Laboratory was founded to explore methodologies and technologies for collective decision-making, and to interrogate the underlying assumptions of legacy systems of governance globally. Designed by Jonathon Keats in collaboration with Steve Durie, Rhonda Holberton and their SJSU students, the first research platform was based on the Federal government of the United States, emulating the decision-making logic by which representatives are selected and legislation is processed. Parameters were adjustable, as was the governing structure. The system was also capable of testing alternative models with actual and hypothetical legislation. For the testing phase, elected officials were simulated with random number generators, where the probabilities of voting outcomes could be set according to the political status quo or anticipated demographics. The legislative emulator and source code were made freely accessible on the Future Democracies Laboratory website, and served as the basis for the simulator used by the Future Democracies Laboratory today.
