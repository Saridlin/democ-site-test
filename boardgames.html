Democracy Board Games @ SJSU

Game workshop with guest artist James Morgan

October 3rd, 2023

Is democracy a game? What might the body politic learn by probing the dynamics of board games such as Battleship and Monopoly? Could new games be collectively designed to generate new modalities of decision-making that might facilitate political consensus? In this hands-on workshop, participants experimentally hacked an assortment of board games created by SJSU students in a game design class taught by James Morgan. All of the games involved decision-making and had rules of play that were designed to be gamed. Through the iterative process of modification, players gained insights into the impact rules have on the outcomes of legislative and voting systems, as well as instincts for determining the affordances and practice at adjusting salient parameters to achieve desired outcomes. The workshop prototyped a process by which all citizens might potentially be involved in the process of democratizing democracy itself.
