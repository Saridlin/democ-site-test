Bananahachi @ SJSU

A collaborative event with CADRE Student Organization

April 29th 2025

Banana Differential Project: Bananahachi Tasting Exhibition

As part of an ongoing exploration into speculative design and eco-feedback systems, the CADRE student organization collaborated with Jonathon Keats and Steve Durie to present a new installment of the Banana Differential Project. This iteration took on a more experimental and participatory form, inviting students not only to revisit the original methods of testing environmental stress through banana growth but to extend the inquiry through culinary creativity.

In this version of the project, students designed and conducted a comparative taste experiment between bananas grown under varying conditions—some exposed to controlled environmental stressors, while others remained as controls. The goal was to see whether subtle changes in the growing environment would manifest as perceptible differences in sweetness, which could potentially serve as indicators of plant stress and adaptation.

What made this experiment unique was the use of recipes as a testing medium. The bananas from each category were incorporated into a range of prepared dishes—banana bread, smoothies, fritters, and more. Each recipe became a data point in the study. Participants were invited to a taste-testing night, where every dish was sampled, rated, and logged for perceived sweetness. Through this shared sensory experience, a speculative yet grounded method emerged: using taste as a qualitative data point for environmental stress.

The results of this collaborative and edible investigation were summarized in a final document (see below), offering insight into how subjective experiences like taste might offer new avenues for environmental sensing and speculative storytelling.
